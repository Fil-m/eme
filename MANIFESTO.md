## The phenomenon that inspires us

- < 0.25% of the ocean → supports ~25% of all marine life
- Symbiosis: polyp + algae = 90% of the coral’s energy from its partner
- Autonomous tiny elements → giant network without a center
- Growth: 0.5–10 cm/year → underwater mountains over millennia

![Colorful thriving reef under sunlight](https://thumbs.dreamstime.com/b/colorful-coral-reef-thriving-sunlight-underwater-paradise-filtering-crystal-clear-ocean-water-illuminates-vibrant-350874084.jpg)

But reefs are fragile: +1–2 °C → mass bleaching in weeks.

![Bleached dead coral close-up](https://c8.alamy.com/comp/F00T7D/close-up-of-a-sunbleached-piece-of-dead-coral-washed-up-on-the-beach-F00T7D.jpg)

This mirrors our world: isolation, burnout, crises.
We want to build **oases of care** exactly where the system fails.

## Our principles — simple and sincere

- **Action matters more than words** — do it, and the world will see
- **Horizontality**: you matter, you decide
- **Transparency**: everything is open
- **Reality over utopia**: functional here and now
- **Self-sufficiency + exchange**: everyone becomes more independent
- **Reproducibility**: easy to repeat in any city, any context
- **Scientific approach**: We apply a scientific approach to the evolution of the network: each action of help is an experiment, the result is recorded as data (what → whom → effect → what worked), analyzed by the community. This allows us to identify patterns that work best and scale them horizontally. To implement changes (both in code and in local initiatives) we use Scrum-inspired cycles: short iterations, daily synchronizations, retrospectives through lessons — all without a center, based on self-organization.

We are not heroes. We are people doing small acts of care for each other.

## Joining is very simple (onboarding in 3–5 minutes)

1. **Read this manifesto.**
2. **Write in the chat** (or digital log):
   “Hi, I’m [name]. I want to be an EME node. Ready to do at least 1 act of support daily. My first contribution: [short about yourself].”
3. **Get a warm welcome** + links to chats/board.
4. **First week**: do 1/1 daily, ask “How can I help?”, invite 1 friend.

No approval needed. You’re already a node.

---

## Daily practice — 1/1 rule + fixing experience

- **Do a small act of support** → in 2–5 min note:
  `What → For whom → Result → What worked / didn’t`
- **Add to the Experience Board** (or Sync with Peers) with `#EMEexperience`.
- **Weekly** (anyone can): top-5 lessons → post in chat.

This is our shared memory — the network learns without a center.

## Care for nodes — the foundation of resilience

- **Right to pause without guilt.**
- **Buddy system**: 1–2 close nodes for check-ins.
- **Channel #EMEsupport**: “I need to talk / help”.
- **Restoration**: at least 1 day/week with no EME activities.
- **Responsibility**: localize consequences + protect nodes.

The network is strong when nodes are alive.
Caring for yourself is caring for everyone.

## Expansion — just copy and do it in your circle

- Invite 1–3 friends weekly (personal message).
- Run an EME circle (offline 3–10 people): intro → share actions → plan help.
- Online: voice circle “What’s on your mind? How can we help?”.
- Create local chat “EME [your city]” + copy templates.

Every node is a seed for a new network.
We grow horizontally, by ourselves.
